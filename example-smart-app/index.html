Endovascular revascularization with percutaneous transluminal angioplasty (PTA) (with or without stent) of the lower extremities (aorto-iliac and superficial femoral arteries only) will be considered medically reasonable and necessary when the following requirements are met: • CLTI when limb-threatening lower extremity (LE) ischemia is present and documented2,3,10,36; OR • Claudication when all of the following requirements have been met: o Inadequate response to three months of optimal guideline-directed medical therapy (GDMT) within the past 12 months2,3; AND o Impairment of activities of daily living and work2,3,10; AND o Individual has undergone a structured exercise program (eg, supervised or home based) 2,3,10; AND o Presence of anatomically suitable lesion for intervention with documentation of any of the following: -Lesion greater than 70% stenosis on angiography (eg, CTA, invasive angiography, MRA)2,32,39; OR -Lesion 50% to 70% stenosis on angiography (eg, CTA, invasive angiography, MRA); AND Resting or provoked intravascular translesional (pre- to post-stenosis) pressure gradient measurement greater than or equal to 10 mmHg2,32,39; OR - LE Duplex doppler with peak systolic velocity (PSV) greater than 300 cm/s or PSV ratio greater than 4.0
